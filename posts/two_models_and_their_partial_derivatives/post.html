While doing some Calculus homework the other day, I came across two exercises that I found pretty interesting. The problems provided data in a tabular format as well as a model (given as a function of x and y). Here is the first of the two:

Expenditures in (billions of dollars) of different types of recreation
x - amusement parks and campgrounds
y - live entertainment (excluding sports)
z - spectator sports
year 2005 2006 2007 2008 2009 2010
x 36.4 39.0 42.4 44.7 43.0 45.2
y 15.3 16.6 17.4 17.5 17.0 17.3
z 16.4 18.1 20.0 20.5 20.1 21.4
  • A model for the data is given by:
      $z = 0.461x + 0.301y - 494$

  • Find $\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}$ and $\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}$.
  • Interpret the partial derivatives in the context of the problem.

I wanted to reproduce this model on my own. So i bust open the R console and painstakingly type in the x, y, and z values. 

    x <- c(36.4,39.0,42.4,44.7,43.0,45.2)
    y <- c(15.3,16.6,17.4,17.5,17.0,17.3)
    z <- c(16.4,18.1,20.0,20.5,20.1,21.4)
    d <- data.frame(x,y,z)
    mdl <- lm(z ~ x + y, data = d)
